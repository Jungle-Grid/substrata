# Validation notes

## Target users

In-house trade compliance managers, export-control analysts, technical reviewers, and advisers supporting semiconductor and advanced hardware companies.

## Buyer and user profiles

Buyers need defensible process, visibility, and auditability. Daily users need a faster way to turn scattered technical material into a review-ready workup.

## Conversations and feedback themes

The recurring themes are document fragmentation, repeated fact extraction, inconsistent precedent handling, and the need to preserve reviewer judgment.

## Company-history insight

A compliance reviewer suggested that prior company decisions and history are critical because new classifications are often evaluated in the context of what the company has reviewed before.

## Key assumptions to validate

- Reviewers will upload and maintain company history when provenance is clear.
- Source-grounded facts and uncertainty flags reduce memo-preparation time.
- Candidate review paths are more useful than an opaque single-answer interface.
- The memo draft fits existing approval workflows.

## What we learned

The company-history wedge is valuable only when it supports comparison without turning precedent into an automatic answer. Human review, citations, and scoped evidence are central to trust.

## Next validation steps

Run structured workflow sessions with compliance teams, measure time-to-first-draft and correction rates, test import quality on real internal formats, and validate what audit/export artifacts buyers require.

## Risks

Source quality, current-control-text interpretation, sensitive-document handling, integration complexity, and over-reliance on generated output all require disciplined product controls.
