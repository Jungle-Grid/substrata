Substrata
Unique insight

The answer is not the product

Companies need a traceable decision process, not only an AI answer.

“A plausible ECCN without evidence lineage, uncertainty, precedent context, and accountable review does not solve the compliance workflow.”

Substrata’s product thesis, informed by product work and early reviewer feedback.
01

Product form before component signals

A gateway containing chips is not classified as if it were only the installed chip. The exported item and component roles must stay distinct.

02

Conflicts must block clean conclusions

Contradictory or missing evidence becomes an explicit uncertainty flag and reviewer task.

03

Company history is context, not authority

Prior decisions improve consistency only when provenance, similarity limits, and current evidence remain visible.

04

Human accountability is part of the system

The reviewer’s decision, rationale, and evidence lineage belong in the same durable record.

Implemented in the canonical evidence/decision model and company-history workflow; see repository documentation.06 / 12

Why now

The cost of manual review is compounding

More restrictions. More complex products. More pressure for fast, defensible decisions.

01

Semiconductor restrictions are expanding

Advanced-computing and semiconductor controls create more review paths, thresholds, and configuration-specific questions for global hardware teams.

02

Products combine more functions

Compute, networking, memory, security, RF, and software capabilities increasingly coexist in one exported product.

03

Defensibility matters as much as speed

Teams need faster preparation without losing source evidence, company context, uncertainty, or qualified human approval.

The opening: turn technical-document review into reusable compliance infrastructure, beginning with datasheet-to-ECCN workups.
