# Compliance boundaries

## Summary

Substrata is a decision-support workspace for review-ready workups. It is not legal advice and does not make final compliance decisions.

## Human review required

Every memo draft, candidate review path, and internal conclusion requires qualified human review and approval.

## Drafts are not final classifications

The system may surface candidate review paths or ECCN-formatted identifiers. Those are review inputs, not approved outcomes.

## Uncertainty and questions

Uncertainty flags and reviewer questions are first-class output. They identify missing technical, configuration, threshold, or source information that can change a review.

## Source citations and audit trail

Facts retain source snippets; review paths retain cited evidence; reviewer actions are recorded in an organization-scoped audit trail.

## Data handling assumptions

Documents and company history are treated as sensitive and organization-scoped. Production hardening still requires managed storage, retention controls, and operational security review.

## What not to use it for

Do not use Substrata as a substitute for qualified export-control judgment or as a reason to publish or ship a product without appropriate review.

## Future controls

Planned controls include stronger retention/deletion policies, managed storage, document quarantine, provider controls, and enterprise access governance.
